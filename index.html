What is Cataract?

A Cataract is a clouding of the normallyclear lens of a human eye. For people who have Cataract, seeing through cloudylenses is a bit like looking through a frosty or fogged-up window. The lens,where Cataract form, is positioned behind the colored part of the eye (iris). The lens focuses light that passes into your eye, producing clear, sharp imageson the retina.

Symptoms of Cataract

Common symptoms of Cataract include:

  • blurry or dim vision
  • reduced-intensity of colors
  • double vision
  • difficulty seeing at nigh
  • sensitivity to light
  • visual halos around bright lights

Causes of Cataract

Common causes of Cataract are:

  • aging
  • eye injury
  • medical conditions such as diabetes
  • previous eye surgery
  • smoking

Diagnosis of Cataract

A thorough eye examination by an ophthalmologist will determine whether a Cataract is present. In the early stages of Cataract development a change in glasses may be all that is needed. A complete eye examination will also determine if there are other causes of vision loss, particularly problems involving the retina or optic nerve.

Treatment of Cataract

The only was to treat Cataract is surgery though is Cataract does not affect or affect vision minimally surgery might not be needed right away. It is recommended by eye specialists to under go Cataract surgery before it starts developing to mature state when patientis left with little or no vision at all and a successful surgery is very difficultto perform at this state. In a Cataract surgery which is a painless procedure, ultrasound wave is used to break up cloudy lens or cloudy lens is replacedby plastic lens called an intraocular lens (IOL).
